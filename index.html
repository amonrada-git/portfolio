Prologue

PHANIN

KMUTT · Bachelor's

A research-driven design project that excavates the lost meanings behind Thai Lao-Vieng tribal textile motifs — translating belief systems into tactile, story-embedded products. Moving beyond aesthetics into cultural memory, the work proves that the most powerful design medium isn't the object, but the scenario it creates.

MOYOGATARI

Tama Art University · Master's

A continued research-driven project exploring how ancient Northern Thai tribal textile motifs can be made meaningful, not just decorative, in modern life. Culminating in MOYOGATARI, a gift tableware brand where motif meaning becomes a vehicle for human connection, the work proves that cultural heritage survives not through preservation, but through context.
